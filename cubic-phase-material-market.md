<p><p>Cubic-phase materials are classified into two primary types: oxides and non-oxides. Oxide cubic-phase materials, such as zirconia and titania, exhibit outstanding thermal and mechanical properties, making them suitable for applications in ceramics, electronics, and catalysis. Non-oxide cubic-phase materials, including carbides and nitrides, are known for their exceptional hardness and thermal stability, often utilized in cutting tools and aerospace components. Both types play crucial roles across various industries, offering unique characteristics tailored to specific technological needs.</p></p>
