<p><p>The welding and shielding gas market comprises several key types, including argon, carbon dioxide, oxygen, and hydrogen. Argon is primarily used for MIG and TIG welding due to its inert nature, providing excellent arc stability. Carbon dioxide, often blended with argon, enhances penetration and reduces spatter in welding. Oxygen is utilized for cutting and can be mixed with other gases to improve flame characteristics. Hydrogen is employed in specific applications, such as in certain types of welding and for reducing metal oxides.</p></p>
