<p><p>The animal vaccination market is primarily categorized into three types: Live Attenuated Vaccines, Inactivated Vaccines, and Other Vaccines. Live attenuated vaccines contain weakened forms of pathogens, eliciting robust immune responses. Inactivated vaccines, on the other hand, use killed pathogens, making them safer but often requiring booster shots for effective immunity. The "Other" category includes subunit vaccines, DNA vaccines, and mRNA vaccines, which utilize specific antigens or genetic material to stimulate an immune response, enhancing safety and effectiveness in preventing diseases.</p></p>
