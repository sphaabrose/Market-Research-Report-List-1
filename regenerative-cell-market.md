<p><ul><li>Allogeneic</li><li>Autologous</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The regenerative cell market is primarily divided into two main types: allogeneic and autologous cells. Allogeneic cells are derived from a donor and can be used in multiple patients, offering scalability and potential for broader applications. In contrast, autologous cells are sourced from the patient themselves, ensuring compatibility and reducing the risk of immune rejection. Both types play crucial roles in regenerative medicine, with allogeneic cells often used for standardized treatments and autologous cells preferred for personalized therapies.</p></p>
