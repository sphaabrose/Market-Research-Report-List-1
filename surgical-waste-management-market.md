<p><p>Surgical waste management involves various methods to safely dispose of medical waste generated during surgeries. Incineration is a high-temperature process that reduces waste to ash, effectively eliminating pathogens but raising environmental concerns. Autoclaving uses steam and pressure to sterilize waste, making it safe for landfilling while minimizing pollution. Chemical disinfection involves treating waste with chemical agents to neutralize harmful pathogens before disposal. Each method addresses different aspects of waste management, prioritizing safety, compliance, and environmental impact.</p></p>
